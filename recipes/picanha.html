Picanha

Sirlon Cap Cut after Resting

Picanha, known but many as "The Queen" is a traditional way of preparing the Sirloin Cap in Brazil. Its a tender, juicy cut very well known for the delicious fat it carries on top. It has now become very popular around the world and its really easy to prepare, as long as you pay attention to the little details.

The preparation is really straightforward and can be cooked in a grill or a pan. As far as tradition goes, it is expected to be prepared on a charcoal grill. With that in mind, lets start with the recipe.

Ingredients and Tools

Ingredients:

  • Whole Sirloin Cap
  • Salt (Better if coarse)

In case you are wondering: yes, it is that simple.

Tools

  • Grill
  • Charcoal
  • Tongs
  • Knive and Cutting Board

Preparation

Now, lets get into actually cooking some picanha

  1. Cut Steaks (Remember to go with the grain)
  2. Dry Brine Overnight
  3. Preheat Grill (Can't hold hand for more than 3seconds)
  4. Place steak directly over the heat
  5. Turn the steaks over as many times as needed, to avoid burning
  6. Keep turning until the skeaks have a nice crust
  7. Check internal temperature (compare to Temp Table)
  8. If desired term is achieved, take the steaks out. If not, keep them at low heat until desired term is achieved
  9. Let each steak rest for about 5minutes. Belive me, its crucial.
  10. Cut perpendicular to the lenght of the steak and enjoy
