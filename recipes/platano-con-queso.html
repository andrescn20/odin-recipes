Grilled Plantain with Cheese

Most certainly, you are familiar with bananas. But, do you know plantains? At first, it looks just like a bigger banana... which it somehow is. Plantains not only are bigger, but also sweeter when ripe. There are plenty of recipes in Latinamerica for both green and ripe plantains.

Taking advantage of its sweetness, a ripe plantain is an amazing dessert when cooked. But theres one level above its already amazing taste: adding some cheese. Together, this ingredients make up for a perfect dessert for our grilled picanha.

Ingredients

  • Ripe Plantains (dont be scared if they seem too ripe)
  • Your preffered Cheese
  • Optional: Berrie Jam

Preparation

This dessert is extremely simple, lets get into it:

  1. Wrap the whole plantains (peel included) in Aluminum foil
  2. Place the plantains near the heat source, but not directly above it
  3. Continue cooking your picanhas and flip the plantains every now and then
  4. While you wait for your picanhas to rest, open up your plantains. They should be cooked.
  5. Turn the steaks over as many times as needed, to avoid burning
  6. Cut parallel to its lengh but only halfway through their thickness
  7. Open up the gap from the cut and fill it up with cheese
  8. Plave the plantains back in the grill at low heat. For better results, put back the grill lid if you have one
  9. After finishing your picanha, your plantains should be ready to serve.
  10. Optional: add some Berrie Jam on top of the cheese
